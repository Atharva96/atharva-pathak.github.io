Strategic Analytics

Product Intelligence Platform

A three-part intelligence system combining competitive web scraping, internal course performance rating, and strategic gap analysis — enabling data-driven decisions on pricing, syllabus design, and new program launches for the executive education business.

Competitive Web Scraping

External data — Understanding what competitors are doing

Built a web scraping engine to extract competitor data from 8+ competitor websites. The scraped data covered course pricing, duration, syllabus structure, faculty details, and enrollment trends — giving the product team a clear picture of the competitive landscape.

What I Built

  • Python scraping scripts using BeautifulSoup & Selenium
  • Automated data extraction from 8+ competitor websites
  • Structured database of competitor pricing, duration, and syllabus
  • Competitor monitoring dashboard in Power BI

How It Was Used

  • Determined optimal pricing for new courses based on competitor benchmarks
  • Identified ideal course durations that competitors were offering
  • Mapped syllabus gaps — topics competitors covered that we didn't
  • Informed go-to-market strategy for new program launches

Course Rating Model

Internal data — Evaluating our own course performance

Each course was rated across Enrollments, Gross Sales Volume, Program Fees, Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC), Cost Per Lead (CPL), completion rates, NPS, and more — guiding investment and discontinuation decisions.

Gap Analysis Matrix

Internal + External data — Finding strategic opportunities

Combined internal course performance data (from the Rating Model) with external competitive data (from Web Scraping) to build a Program Matrix. This matrix identified white spaces — high-demand topics competitors offered that we didn't, underperforming courses that needed redesign, and pricing/duration mismatches vs. market standards.

What It Identified

  • High-demand topics competitors offered that we didn't
  • Courses with high CAC but low enrollment — candidates for discontinuation
  • Pricing gaps where we were significantly above or below market
  • Duration mismatches vs. what the market was offering

Decisions It Enabled

  • 6 new programs launched based on white space identification
  • 3 underperforming programs redesigned with updated syllabus
  • Pricing recalibrated for 15+ courses to match market positioning
  • Presented findings to Company MD and Senior Management

Why I Built This

The executive education market was evolving rapidly but decisions on pricing, syllabus, and new launches were based on intuition rather than data. We had no systematic way to monitor competitors, evaluate our own courses objectively, or identify strategic gaps.

What I Built

  • Course Rating Model with 25 internal parameters in Advanced Excel
  • Program Gap Matrix combining internal + external intelligence
  • Dashboards and strategic presentations for leadership
